Hangul

The Korean alphabet is actually very easy to learn when compared to alphabets from others such as Chinese and Japanese. As Korean as it requires little memorisation, the following sections will teach you the Hangul and romanized versions of the consonants, vowels as well as the basic combinations and the additional rules. Once you understand Hangul you should then avoid looking at romanized versions of words, the romanizations used below aim to be the best representations using English but you should use audio of the characters and words ensure accurate pronunciation.

Blocks

Korean is made up of blocks which also act as syllables, these are made up of the individual characters. These blocks can be constructed in different ways as summarised below. The first element is always a consonant which can also be pronounced silently or be a double consonant as you will learn further on. Next will be either one or two vowels below or to the right inside of the block, finally sometimes there will be a final consonant at the bottom of the block.

Additional Rules or Outliers

Additionally there are additional rules or outliers to the language as summarised below.

ㅇ(ng) will be silent and not pronounced if placed in the initial position of a block and required if vowel is being placed at the front of a block/syllable, otherwise it is pronounced normally. For example 엄마 is pronounced eomma with the ㅇ(ng) being silent, while 강 is pronounced kang as ㅇ is in the final position.

There are also some consonants that if placed in the final position within a block will be pronounced with ㅌ(t) sound, these characters are ㄷ, ㅅ, ㅆ, ㅈ, ㅊ, and ㅌ. For example 맛 is pronounced as mat, 꽃 pronounced as got, and 있다 pronounced as itda.
