Hangul

The Korean alphabet is actually very easy to learn compared to alphabets from other languages such as Chinese and Japanese. As Korean as it requires little memorisation, the following sections will teach you the Hangul and romanized versions of the consonants, vowels as well as the basic combinations and the additional outlier combinations. Once you understand Hangul you should then avoid looking at romanized versions of words, the romanizations used below aim to be the best representations using English but you should instead use audio of the words and practice with them to ensure accurate pronunciation.

Blocks

Korean is made up of blocks that each contain a syllable but can be drawn in different ways as summarised below. The first element is a consonant which can also be pronounced silently or be a double consonant as you will learn further on. Next will be either one or two vowels below or to the right of the block, finally sometimes there will be a final consonant at the bottom of the block.

Consonants

Below are all of the basic Korean consonants, most of which are very straightforward as most are unique with the only ㅈ(j) and ㅊ(ch) being similar. In terms of their pronunciation only ㄹ is difficult as it sounds like a mix between r and l, and may require time to get used to.

Another part that will require remembering is that ㅇ(ng) will be silent and not pronounced if placed in the initial position of a block and required if vowel is being placed at the front of a block/syllable, otherwise it is pronounced normally. For example 엄마 is pronounced eomma with the ㅇ(ng) being silent, while 강 is pronounced kang as ㅇ is in the final position.

Additional Rules or Outliers

Additionally there are additional rules or outliers to the language as summarised below.

ㅇ(ng) will be silent and not pronounced if placed in the initial position of a block and required if vowel is being placed at the front of a block/syllable, otherwise it is pronounced normally. For example 엄마 is pronounced eomma with the ㅇ(ng) being silent, while 강 is pronounced kang as ㅇ is in the final position.

There are also some consonants that if placed in the final position within a block will be pronounced with ㅌ(t) sound, these characters are ㄷ, ㅅ, ㅆ, ㅈ, ㅊ, and ㅌ. For example 맛 is pronounced as mat, 꽃 pronounced as got, and 있다 pronounced as itda.
